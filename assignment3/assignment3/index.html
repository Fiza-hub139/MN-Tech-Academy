Chicken Biryani

Biryani is a celebrated, fragrant rice dish that originated among the Muslims of the Indian subcontinent, combining long-grain rice (typically basmati) with spices, saffron, and meat, eggs, or vegetables. Renowned for its complex, layered flavors, it is considered a centerpiece of South Asian culinary heritage, often featured at weddings, festivals, and special occasions

INGREGIENTS List:

  • Rice
  • Bone-in chicken
  • Yogurt
  • Onions
  • Ginger-garlic paste

    • A blend of whole and ground spices:

    • Bay leaf
    • Cardamom
    • Cloves
    • Cinnamon
    • Cumin
    • Garam masala
    • Turmeric
    • Chili powder
    • Salt

      Extra things for toppings or others :

    • Raeeta
    • Salad
    • Coriander Leaf

Procedure

  1. Marinate: Mix chicken with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, spices, lemon juice, half the fried onions, mint, and coriander. Marinate for at least 30 mints.

  2. Prepare Rice: Boil rice with whole spices (bay leaf, cardamom, cloves) until it is 70%-90% cooked. Drain and set aside

  3. Cook Chicken: In a heavy-bottomed pot, heat ghee and cook the marinated chicken over medium heat for about 10-15 minutes until it is mostly cooked.

  4. Layering: Spread cooked chicken at the bottom, top with half the rice, fried onions, mint, and coriander. Repeat layers. Top with remaining fried onions and saffron milk.

  5. Dum Cooking: Cover tightly with a lid and cook on very low heat for 15-20 minutes.

  6. Rest & Serve: Let the biryani rest for 10-15 minutes before opening to allow the steam to set the flavors. Fluff gently and serve.
